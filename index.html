How did this happen??

February 2011

A a bored George, about to move to Southern Georgia, decides to do a global search for "best match" on a popular dating site. On the first page of results is a woman with almost the same username (a reference to the well-known computer scientist Edsger Dijkstra). He decides to send a message:

Feb 2, 2011 - 6:44pm
I just wanted to apologize for all the quick-and-dirty hacks I've ever committed. (I didn't know any better; I'm only an engineer.)

Meanwhile...

One of Elaine's best friends, tired of the single life, has decided to sign up for a popular online dating site, but not alone. She badgers Elaine into making an account as well. Less than two weeks later, Elaine receives a message from a mysterious engineer with nearly the same username as her. Sensing someone who might be up for some banter (and, ugh, he's cute too), she replies:

Feb 2, 2011 - 8:02pm
Oh, I know about you engineers -- I'm related to one and he writes the most painful-to-read code ever (why are ALL your variables global???) [Ed.: Sorry, Dale!] . On the bright side, engineers are much less likely to get caught up in "I will implement this in the most general way possible" and end up only finishing half of what they set out to do (not that I have ever done such a thing, of course).

A few weeks (and one nail-bitingly long gap in George's messages, thanks to that move) later:

At this point we know:

  1. Elaine and George both enjoy pretentious references, snappy wit, grandiose vocabulary, and all things computer-related.
  2. Despite living 2,500 miles apart on opposite sides of the country, their hometowns are less than a five-hour's drive apart in Pennsylvania and Upstate New York.
  3. Elaine is going to be in Georgia for the summer for a research internship and George owes her a drink (in exchange for the rights to use one of her cleverer lines).
  4. ... but George is going to be underway for the entire summer; there's no way they can meet up while Elaine is in Georgia.
  5. They can't quite bring themselves to let this chance slip away.

April 2011

Fortunately for everyone involved, Elaine has a bit of a taste for adventure, and suggests meeting up in Austin, Texas, almost exactly halfway between Los Angeles and Saint Marys. Two rooms are reserved at a grad-student-budget-friendly motel on the outskirts of downtown Austin, plane tickets are purchased, and April 1st, 2011 (yes, April Fool's Day) finds Elaine sitting outside a gate in the Delta terminal of the Austin airport, frantically drawing robots in a futile attempt to calm her nerves.

George steps off the plane looking even more engineer-ish than usual, but Elaine is charmed. They hold hands in the taxi to the hotel, then walking around Austin the next day. Less than a week after their trip, they decide to give a relationship a try.

March 2015

After four years, sixty thousand frequent flier miles, five 3-month stretches underway, more than 400 emails, endless video calls and chatting, one cross-country move, a shared lease, and two cats, they decide it's time to get married.
